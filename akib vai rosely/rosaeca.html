Solution for Rosacea

Rosacea is considered as a chronic, benign inflammatory skin condition that usually affects the face. If left untreated, the condition may worsens with time. Pink or red facial skin, small red bumps sometimes containing cysts and pus, irritated eyes and small dilated blood vessels are the most common signs and symptoms of Rosacea. 
The redness gradually spreads beyond the cheeks and nose to the chin and forehead. Sometimes the chest and ears become also red. An individual having this kind of skin disorder just assume that he/she has very sensitive skin that flushes or blushes easily. The redness may cause permanent enlargement of small blood vessels of the face, as a result of which they become more visible and appearing like telangiectasia’s (tiny red lines). 
Continual episodes of blushing and flushing leads to inflammation, causing small papules or red bumps just like acne. Overgrowth of dermal tissues is one of the most unpleasant aspects of rosacea, due to which phymatous change produce in the skin. This appears as permanent swelling and thickening of facial tissues. Rhinophyma (a bulbous nose) is an example of this change.
WHAT CAUSES ROSACEA?
Scientists are still not sure about actual causes of rosacea. Its main cause is not clearly known, but it may be occurred due to environmental, or hereditary factors. By studying deeply, scientists have found some important factors and clues that are thought to contribute in the disorder:
FACIAL BLOOD VESSEL ABNORMALITIES: Abnormalities in the facial blood vessels leads to persistent redness, flushing, and visible blood vessels.
FAIR COMPLEXION: People with fair complexion have a much higher percentage of developing the disorder.
A MICROSCOPIC MITE (Demodex folliculorum):  Demodex folliculorum is a tiny insect, that lives on everyone’s skin usually on the cheeks, and the nose and, it is not harmful. However, it is considered that rosacea patients possess higher number of these mites than others. But it is still unclear whether rosacea is caused by mites or it spreads the population of mites.


H. pylori BACTERIUM: H. pylori, a bacterium usually presents in the gut, responsible to stimulate bradykinin production, which is known to cause the dilation of blood vessels. Some scientists suggested that the bacterium plays an important role in development of rosacea. 
FAMILY HISTORY (INHERITANCE, GENES): It might be possible that individual having rosacea inherit genes from their parents or closed relatives.
Rosacea may be aggravated by some other factors including:
 
Humidity, sunlight or wind
Cosmetics
Vigorous exercise
Hot drinks and foods
Spicy foods, caffeine or dairy products
High temperatures
Alcohol consumption
Anxiety, stress, embarrassment, anger
And some medications




ROLE OF MEDICATIONS IN TREATMENT OF ROSACEA: 
Depending on the extent and severity of symptoms, there are many treatment choices for this chronic skin disorder. Some lifestyle changes and few medications may help in improving the symptoms. Available treatments include: Topical creams, lasers, antibacterial washes, pulsed light therapies, photo dynamic therapy, and antibiotic pills.
TOPICAL MEDICATIONS: Treatment with topical medications is considered as one of the popular method. Common topical antibiotic medications that are used to control bumps, and redness in rosacea include:
metronidazole (Metro gel)
(Azelex cream, Finacea gel 15%)
Sodium sulfacetamide (Klaron lotion)
erythromycin and 
clindamycin (Cleocin)
Ivermectin cream (Soolantra Cream, 1%)
All of these above medications are extremely useful in improving the symptoms of rosacea by applying once or twice a day.
ORAL ANTIBIOTICS: Patients suffering from moderate rosacea are usually prescribed with oral antibiotic medications, that are useful in reducing inflammation. The most common oral antibiotics are:Tetracycline (Sumycin),
 doxycycline (Vibramycin, Oracea, Adoxa, Atridox), 
 minocycline (Dynacin, Minocin),
Doxycycline
ISOTRETINOIN(ACCUTANE): Isotretinoin is only prescribed for resistant and severe rosacea.  It is an acne drug that is used to treat acne vulgaris (common acne).
OTHER TREATMENTS:
LASER AND INTENSE LIGHT TREATMENT: Majority of patients are now turning towards intense and laser light therapies to treat redness and inflammation on the neck, chest and face. Intense and laser pulse light therapy also help to improve complexion and skin. Sometimes the treatment can cause crusting of skin, bruising, and tenderness but these complications usually disappear within a week.PHOTODYNAMIC THERAPY: It is new treatment for rosacea. It uses Chemical photo sensitizing agent along with light that activates sensitizer. The treatment has proven the ability to kill fungi, viruses, bacteria and microbial cells. PDT is most commonly used in the treatment of acne.
TIPS FOR MANAGING SYMPTOMS: Along with medications, these lifestyle and home remedies may also help in curing the symptoms of rosacea:


PUT ON SCREEN: Always wear a wide hat, and 3D sunglasses to cover your face while going outside.
USE ONLY GENTLE SKINCARE PRODUCTS; Do not use skincare products having alcohol, witch hazel, fragrance and other harsh ingredients. Only use soft towel or cloth after washing your face.
USE A MOISTURIZER: In low temperature and wind, your skin become dry. Use good quality moisturizers to avoid this dryness.
MASSAGE YOUR FACE: Rubbing your face gently in aa circular motion can also be a good tip for managing the symptoms.
USE OF NON COMEDOGENC PRODUCTS: These kinds of products do not block oil and sweat gland openings that is why they are considered as useful skincare products.
AVOID OVER THE COUNTER (OTC) STEROID CREAMS: Do not use these creams without prescription of your dermatologist because they may worsen the symptoms.
